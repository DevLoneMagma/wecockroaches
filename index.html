"Unemployed youth who cannot find jobs run to media and RTI activism to attack the system. Like cockroaches."

— Chief Justice of India

उसने गाली दी। हमने पार्टी बना ली।

From the highest bench in the country, the Chief Justice compared job-seeking youth to cockroaches. His logic: if you're unemployed and asking questions, you're a pest. A nuisance. Something to be fumigated.

He forgot one thing. Cockroaches outlived the dinosaurs. They survive nuclear winters. They live in the walls of every VIP bungalow. They cannot be exterminated. And when you try — they multiply.

So we took the name. We built the movement. Not as a registered political party. As a swarm of students, teachers, farmers, journalists, RTI activists — every person the system has tried to crush and failed.

You're here because you already know which one you are.

Our Demands

Manifesto of
the Unkillable जो नहीं टूटते उनकी माँगें

01

No Post-Retirement Rajya Sabha

CJI retire हों — तो घर जाएँ, Rajya Sabha नहीं

Any Chief Justice who retires shall be ineligible for any political appointment for 10 years. Judicial independence is not a talking point — it's a structural requirement. You cannot be the highest arbiter of the constitution one day and a ruling-party legislator the next.

02

Every Deleted Vote is a Criminal Act

एक भी vote delete हुआ तो Election Commission जवाब देगा

Systematic deletion of legitimate voters from electoral rolls shall be prosecuted as electoral fraud. No bureaucratic error. No "technical glitch." Voting is a constitutional right. Deleting it is a constitutional crime.

03

50% Women. Not 33. Fifty.

आधी आबादी को आधा हक — पूरा, not charity

Half the population will not accept a third of democracy. 50% reservation in Parliament and Cabinet. Because representation is not a concession — it is a right. The Women's Reservation Bill gave us 33% in 2047. We want 50%, now.

04

Audit Every Prime Time Anchor

जो सच न बोले, उसके bank account की जाँच हो

Media houses receiving political funding while claiming editorial independence shall face mandatory public audit. Any anchor running government propaganda as journalism during prime time should be able to answer one question: who is paying for the studio, and what are they getting in return?

05

Anti-Defection: 20 Years, No Exceptions

Party छोड़ी तो 20 साल के लिए राजनीति बंद

Any elected MLA or MP who switches parties loses the right to contest elections or hold public office for 20 years. You were elected on a mandate — not a temp contract. If you want to join another party, first return the seat you won on someone else's name.

06

RTI is a Right. Not a Nuisance.

RTI activist hero हैं — cockroach नहीं, जनाब

Every RTI application must be answered within the statutory 30 days. Obstruction, delay, or intimidation of RTI applicants shall carry mandatory criminal penalties. The Right to Information is how citizens hold power accountable. That's not a nuisance — that's democracy working correctly.

The Record

Hall of
Shame

SHAME / 001
Demo-
netization

8PM. No warning. ₹500 and ₹1000 notes — cancelled. ATMs ran dry for months. 100+ people died in queues. When it was over, 99.3% of the banned currency had returned to banks. The black money it was supposed to destroy was in gold, benami property, and foreign accounts — untouched. The poor lost savings. The rich lost nothing.

"Kuch dino ki takleef hai." The queues lasted 90 days. Some people didn't leave them.

SHAME / 002
Pegasus
Snooping

Military-grade Israeli spyware found on phones of journalists, judges, opposition leaders, and activists. Government response: neither confirm nor deny. Parliament response: disrupted session. Investigation: none ordered. The state watched dissent in real time — and found nothing worth stopping, apparently, except the people doing the watching.

"Agar kuch galat nahi kiya toh darr kya?" They watched everything. Found no terrorists. Kept watching.

SHAME / 003
Electoral
Bonds

Seven years. Unlimited anonymous corporate donations to political parties, through a government bank that privately shared records only with the government. Supreme Court: unconstitutional. The money: already spent. The contracts: already awarded. The scheme that was sold as "transparent funding" turned out to be the most sophisticated legalized corruption in Indian electoral history.

"It's not a scam if it's technically legal." It was unconstitutional. But the money's gone.

SHAME / 004
The Bridge
Files

Morbi: 135 dead. Machar: structural failure two months after renovation. Mizoram rail: 23 dead mid-construction. Every bridge — tender irregularities. Every investigation — stalled. Every contractor — connected. India has a word for this: jugaad. The engineering marvel is not the bridges. It's that anyone crosses them with confidence.

"Quality assured tha." The CCTV footage, like the contractor, is unavailable.
